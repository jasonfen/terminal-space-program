## Slice 2 — 2-stage Lander split + surface staging event

<!-- llm-parse: slice=v0.12.x weight=M-L scope=spacecraft+sim+bodies+tui status=designed backlog=lander-2-stage adr=0007 -->

**Backlog ID:** [`lander-2-stage`](state-of-game.md#2-stage-lander-descent--ascent-split)
(added during the v0.11.5 grill).

**Committed scope.** The real Apollo LM was two vehicles: an ascent
stage that launched *from* the descent stage on the surface, leaving
the descent stage as discarded hardware on the lunar surface. v0.11.5
keeps the Lander a single catalog stage. v0.12 splits it:

- New catalog stages `lander-descent` + `lander-ascent` (replacing
  the single Lander loadout stage; descent inherits the v0.11.5
  silhouette tuning — legs, hypergolic flame, squat body).
- A **surface staging event**: decouple the ascent stage from the
  descent stage *while Landed*, leaving the descent stage as a Landed
  wreck on the surface and the ascent stage as the player's active
  craft for return-to-lunar-orbit.
- A new lifecycle moment between Touchdown and Re-launch — this is a
  *gameplay* extension, not a visual one.

**ADR 0007 authored** ([`docs/adr/0007-surface-staging-and-decouple-plan.md`](adr/0007-surface-staging-and-decouple-plan.md),
2026-05-29 grill). Resolved: the abandoned descent stage is a **Landed
passive** Vessel (not Crashed, not a new state — no schema bump); the
post-decouple re-fuse is prevented by a `checkDocking` guard that skips
any pair where **both** craft are Landed (deferring moon-base landed-
docking to its own feature); `StageActive` **generalises** — the
chain-pop is shared and only the jettisoned-craft *placement* branches
on `parent.Landed` (surface = pinned at the landed lat/lon; orbital =
retrograde nudge). The slice grew to **M–L**: the Apollo Stack LM is
split too, which required an **explicit per-loadout Decouple Plan**
(`Spacecraft.DecouplePlan []int`, group sizes consumed positionally;
Apollo = `[1,1,1,2]`; `decouple_plan,omitempty` additive on the save
wire — no `SchemaVersion` bump). Both descent and ascent stages carry
`CanSoftLand=true`.

**Deps.** v0.11.1 all-vessels-in-SOI (the abandoned descent stage must
render its own sprite via the LUT pipeline) + v0.11.4 Crashed/Landed
lifecycle (both shipped).

## Slice 3 — Parachutes for atmospheric descent + recovery

<!-- llm-parse: slice=v0.12.x weight=L scope=physics+spacecraft+sim+tui status=committed backlog=parachutes adr=0008 -->

**Backlog ID:** [`parachutes`](state-of-game.md#parachutes-for-atmospheric-descent--recovery).
ADR 0004 explicitly preserved this hook so capsule-class vessels (the
historical parachute users) get a non-`CanSoftLand` route to surviving
entry.

**Committed scope.** A non-engine path to soft Touchdown — a deployed
parachute on a Vessel *without* `CanSoftLand` qualifies for the
surface-arrival predicate via aerodynamic deceleration. Touches three
subsystems:

- **Drag** (`internal/physics/drag.go`): a deploy-time
  `BallisticCoefficient` bump — a deployed chute massively increases
  drag area. The existing exponential-ρ(h) drag model (v0.8.4) is the
  substrate.
- **Spacecraft state**: a parachute deploy flag + deployed-vs-torn
  states (over-speed deploy tears the chute).
- **Control modes**: a deploy keystroke gated on dynamic pressure +
  altitude (deploy too fast → torn; deploy too high → no atmosphere).
- **Touchdown predicate**: extend the v0.11.4 Crashed/Soft-Landed
  predicate at the `physics.ClampToSurface` site so a chute-decelerated
  vessel under `V_CRIT` qualifies for Landed even without `CanSoftLand`.

**ADR 0008 needed.** New vessel subsystem with its own state machine
(stowed → deployed → torn), and a second non-engine route into the
Landed predicate. Document the deploy-gate thresholds (q-max for tear,
min altitude) + the BC-bump model. This is the L item — if the cycle
runs long, this is the natural one to split into its own follow-on
tag or slip to v0.13.

**Deps.** v0.8.4 drag + v0.11.4 Crashed/Landed (both shipped).
Independent of slices 1, 2, 4, 5.

## Slice 5 — Combined plane-shift + Hohmann — ✓ SHIPPED (v0.12.1)

<!-- llm-parse: slice=v0.12.1 weight=XL scope=planner+sim+spacecraft+save+tui status=shipped shipped-in=v0.12.1 pr=65 backlog=plane-shift-hohmann adr=0005 grilled=2026-05-28 -->

> **Shipped v0.12.1** (`bf2ff4e`, PR #65). Two latent bugs surfaced in
> playtest and were corrected in the **Slice 5 follow-on** (v0.12.2,
> below). See that section + ADR 0006.

**Backlog ID:** [`plane-shift-hohmann`](state-of-game.md#combined-plane-shift--hohmann)
— **spec-committed** (was targeted v0.11, slipped). **Designed
2026-05-28 via `/grill-with-docs`**; see
[ADR 0005](adr/0005-combined-plane-shift-hohmann-via-lambert.md) for
the decision record. Scope frozen below.

**Frozen design (8 decisions resolved in the grill).**

1. **Scope: intra-primary `[H]` only.** Fix the LEO→Luna path
   (`PlanTransfer` → `PlanIntraPrimaryHohmann`), the one the guard
   flags. Heliocentric (already Lambert) and moon-escape are out of
   scope. *(Q1)*
2. **Architecture: fused Lambert, not analytic.** Replace the
   rendezvous branch of `PlanIntraPrimaryHohmann` with a single-rev
   Lambert solve from the craft's actual state to the target's actual
   arrival position. The 3D departure velocity carries eccentricity +
   raise + plane change together — so **"eccentric-aware departure"
   isn't a separate deliverable, it's free** (no vis-viva / periapsis
   placement; no node-vs-periapsis conflict). *(Q2)*
3. **Phasing: Hohmann-seeded single Lambert.** Seed the departure
   window + TOF from the existing analytic phasing (synodic wait +
   half-ellipse TOF), then one Lambert solve; optional small TOF
   root-find to refine. Reuses tested phasing math. *(Q3)*
4. **Δv-split: build both, auto-pick cheaper.** Compute the combined
   fused-Lambert transfer AND a split alternative (near-coplanar raise
   + `BurnPlaneChange` node at the transfer apoapsis, where the plane
   change is cheapest), plant whichever has lower total Δv, show both
   numbers in the HUD. Large departure inclinations favour the split.
   *(Q4)*
5. **Predictor fidelity, in-slice.** The Lambert solve yields the
   encounter time + target, so densify predicted-trajectory sample
   density (and fine body-position refresh) in the known encounter
   window — the dashed line draws the capture arc. (v0.10.3 capped the
   sub-step so it stops *skipping* the SOI; the even sample budget
   still put too few points in Luna's brief pass.) Avoids the
   "looks like it still misses" playtest trap. *(Q5, Q6)*
6. **`BurnVector` burn mode (new).** Carries the arbitrary 3D fused
   departure Δv that no existing mode can express. Plant-only;
   `ManeuverNode.BurnDirUnit Vec3` (omitempty) captures a unit
   direction in the inertial frame at plant-time. Reuses
   `ThrustAccelFnFixedDir` + the v0.10.0 slew system (lead-compensated
   like any node). New CONTEXT.md glossary entry under **Burn Mode**.
   *(Q7)*
7. **Guard retirement.** Re-point `HohmannDepartureWarning` to fire
   only on genuine Lambert non-convergence / degenerate geometry;
   delete the obsolete "match plane `[I]` + circularize, then `[H]`"
   advisory text. *(consolidated)*
8. **`I` tools kept.** `PlanPlaneMatch` / `PlanInclinationChange` stay
   as standalone manual maneuvers — no longer prerequisite to `[H]`,
   not deprecated. Update the `?` help overlay + README. Multi-rev
   stays a heliocentric-porkchop feature; the auto-plant uses
   single-rev N=0, short branch. *(consolidated)*

**Held out of scope** *(Q8)*: routing the heliocentric Lambert
departure through `BurnVector` (it currently fudges departure as pure
prograde by radius-sign). Nearly-free once `BurnVector` exists, but
deferred to its own slice + playtest to keep this XL slice bounded.

**Save.** Additive `ManeuverNode.BurnDirUnit` (+ `ActiveBurn` mirror)
+ new `BurnMode`, omitempty — follows the `CurrentAttitudeDir`
schema-v6 precedent. Old saves have no `BurnVector` nodes → no
migration.

**Test surface.** Eccentric coplanar departure matches an independent
two-body propagation; inclined departure arrives in the target plane
within tolerance; the old `I`-then-`H` dance and the combined path
converge to the same orbit; the auto-pick selects the split for a
large-Δi case and the combined for a small-Δi case; `BurnVector` node
round-trips through save/load; the predicted trajectory's encounter
window contains the SOI capture (sample density assertion).

**Weight: XL.** Fused Lambert + intra-primary phasing + a new burn
mode + slew interaction + the dual-strategy planner + a predictor-
fidelity rewrite. **Natural split point if it runs long:** ship the
combined-only planner + `BurnVector` + predictor fix first, add the
split-strategy comparison (decision 4) as a follow-on tag.

**Why independent.** Planner/sim-only; no dependency on slices 2–4.
The lowest-ADR-risk meaty slice (ADR 0005 already written) — grill
done, ready for TDD.

## Slice 5 follow-on — transfer arrival + Projected Orbit fidelity (GH #66/#67)

<!-- llm-parse: slice=v0.12.2 weight=M scope=sim+planner status=shipped shipped-in=v0.12.2 adr=0006 issues=66,67 pr=69 grilled=2026-05-29 -->

**Why.** v0.12.1 shipped Slice 5 with two latent bugs, found by
diagnosis 2026-05-29 (exact-Kepler measurement) and confirmed in
playtests — see [ADR 0006](adr/0006-intra-primary-transfer-arrival-and-predictor-fidelity.md),
GH [#66](https://github.com/jasonfen/terminal-space-program/issues/66)
(Projected Orbit) and [#67](https://github.com/jasonfen/terminal-space-program/issues/67)
(transfer arrival). The auto-picked split misses an inclined Luna by
~100 000 km (plane change at an arbitrary apoapsis, not on the Line of
Nodes), and the Projected Orbit's coast leg drifts ~46 000 km
(fixed-step Verlet on the e≈0.96 ellipse). **Acceptance bar (the
load-bearing requirement, not Δv):** `[H]` to an inclined Luna plants a
transfer that arrives within Luna's SOI at ≈0° relative inclination and
captures, and the Projected Orbit draws that capture.

**Verified feasible (grill prototype, 2026-05-29):** the node-placed
split lands exactly on Luna's orbit ring (Δ 0 km), 0.000° relative
inclination, for ~3 930 m/s (3060 raise + 64 node plane change + 806
capture). The combined Lambert is a dead end for inclined targets
(arrives 21–74° steep, 10–14 km/s) — not auto-picked there.

**Part A — Projected Orbit fidelity (#66).** In `internal/sim/predict.go`
`PredictedSegmentsFrom`, propagate ballistic coast legs with **Kepler
Step** (extend the existing Propagation Mode; exact for bound two-body
arcs), falling back to Verlet only inside an atmosphere (drag) or on
hyperbolic arcs (e≥1). Mirror the live `canKeplerStep` guards. SOI
transitions stay caller-handled via the existing per-sub-step
`FindPrimary`/`Rebase`. Fold in sample concentration around the
SOI-entry window so the now-correct arc is drawn smoothly. *Test:* the
coast-leg samples match an exact-Kepler arc within tight tolerance; the
predicted path enters Luna's SOI (foreign segment present).

**Part B — Split arrives coplanar (#67).** Replace
`splitPlaneChangeAtApoapsis` + phasing with a Line-of-Nodes
construction: constrain the transfer so apoapsis falls on the
craft-plane ∩ Luna-plane node, and phase so Luna is at that node at
arrival (1-D timing solve — Luna crosses each node ~twice per orbit).
Plane change at that node → coplanar arrival. The combined path is
demoted to near-coplanar / general use only. *Test:* the planted
transfer's arrival state is within Luna's SOI at ≈0° relative
inclination. *(Combined's near-180° conditioning is tracked in #67 as a
separate, lower-priority item.)*

**✓ Shipped · v0.12.2 (PR [#69](https://github.com/jasonfen/terminal-space-program/pull/69)).**
Part A: `PredictedSegmentsFrom` + the sibling `propagateStateWithPrimary`
(a second Verlet-drift site) now propagate coast legs with `KeplerStep`,
gated by a shared `canKeplerStepState`; the coast-leg samples match an
exact Kepler arc and the predicted path draws the Luna SOI encounter.
Part B: `splitNodePhasing` places the transfer apoapsis on the Line of
Nodes and phases the target there at arrival — the inclined LEO→Luna
rendezvous (21 152 km, inside Luna's SOI). The plane change and capture,
previously stacked at the apoapsis instant (which the executor can't fly
and which fired inside the SOI), now fire as distinct burns: the plane
change just before SOI entry (Earth frame, 19.44°→0.65° transfer tilt)
and the capture at perilune. **Deferred (GH [#68](https://github.com/jasonfen/terminal-space-program/issues/68)):**
the *lunar capture* orbit still arrives ~12–17° inclined / retrograde —
the Moon-relative approach geometry needs a Moon-frame maneuver the
transfer side can't provide; accepted as a large improvement over the
prior 70°+ tilts, to grill later.

**Weight: M.** Sim + planner; no save/UI schema change. Part A and B are
independent and individually shippable; B is the harder math (Line-of-
Nodes phasing). Both gate on the behavioural arrival/capture test, not
Δv. **Verify:** run the app, fly `[H]` to Luna, confirm the dashed
Projected Orbit curves into Luna's SOI and the capture is coplanar.
